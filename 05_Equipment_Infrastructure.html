Bulk Fuel Tanks (T13–T100)

Available in sizes from 13KL (T13) to 100KL (T100). Fully portable with forklift pockets or lifting points. Manufactured from new steel to AS1692 and AS1940 standards.

  • T13 (13,000L) Compact portable unit. Forklift pockets. Ideal for small remote sites.
  • T25 (25,000L) Mid-range portable unit. Forklift pockets or lifting points.
  • T50 (50,000L) Large capacity. Skid-mounted. Ideal for medium to large operations.
  • T80 (80,000L) High capacity. Skid-mounted. Suitable for high-volume remote sites.
  • T100 (100,000L) Maximum capacity. Skid-mounted. For the largest remote operations.
  • Standards AS1692 (Steel tanks for flammable liquids) + AS1940 (Storage and handling of flammable liquids)
  • Construction New steel. Epoxy primer and polyurethane topcoat. Lockable pump bay.

Combination Tanks (Diesel + AdBlue®)

Smart Safety & Control Features

Every IOR self-bunded tank comes with a comprehensive suite of safety and control features — designed to meet Australian standards and protect your operation.

  • Lockable Pump Bay Integral lockable pump bay for secure fuel dispensing.
  • Overfill Alarm Audible and visual overfill alarm. Prevents spills and environmental incidents.

Frequently Asked Questions

Fuel Storage Tanks — common questions.

IOR supplies self-bunded tanks from T13 (13,000L) to T100 (100,000L) and combination tanks up to 200,000L. All tanks are double-walled (self-bunded) for AS 1940 compliance and are available with integrated dispensing equipment and HyDip™ FMS monitoring.

A self-bunded tank has an inner tank and an outer tank (bund) built as a single unit. The outer bund contains 110% of the inner tank capacity, providing secondary containment without the need for a separate concrete bund. This makes self-bunded tanks faster to install and more cost-effective for remote sites.

Yes. IOR supplies tanks for diesel, aviation fuel (Jet A-1 and Avgas), and AdBlue. Combination tanks with separate compartments for multiple products are also available. Download the capability brochures above for full specifications.

Yes. All IOR tanks are compatible with HyDip™ FMS, which provides real-time level monitoring, automated low-level alerts, and delivery scheduling integration. HyDip™ can also be retrofitted to existing tanks.

A frac tank is a large, portable storage tank used for temporary or semi-permanent liquid storage on project sites. IOR's frac tank range includes Container Frac Tanks (45ft skidded), Mobile Frac Tanks (500bbl), and Open-Top DST Tanks (180bbl/400bbl) for use in mining, civil construction, and oil & gas applications.

Frac tanks are designed for temporary or mobile use — they can be transported to site, deployed quickly, and relocated when no longer needed. Permanent self-bunded tanks are installed for long-term, fixed-site storage. IOR can advise on the right solution for your project timeline and budget.
