Common Questions

Everything you need to know about on-site refuelling with IOR.

IOR owns and maintains the tank. There is zero capital outlay for the customer. We install, insure, service, and replace the infrastructure — you simply use it.
Minimum volumes vary by site type and location. Our team will assess your operation and recommend the right solution for your consumption profile. Contact us to discuss your requirements.
All IOR self-bunded tanks are fully compliant with AS 1940 (storage and handling of flammable and combustible liquids) and relevant state environmental regulations. Secondary containment is built in — no bunded compound required.
Yes. HyDip™ is a modular system that can be retrofitted to most existing tanks. Our team will assess compatibility and provide a retrofit quote. You don’t need to replace your infrastructure to get live monitoring and secure dispensing.
Lead times vary depending on tank size, site access, and location. In most cases, standard installations are completed within 2–4 weeks of approval. Remote or complex sites may require additional planning time. Contact us to discuss your timeline.

Frequently Asked Questions

On-Site Refuelling — common questions.

On-site refuelling means IOR installs and manages a fuel storage tank at your site, keeping it stocked and monitored so your equipment never runs dry. IOR owns the infrastructure — there is no capital outlay for your business.

On-site refuelling is most commonly used by mining and resources, civil construction, agriculture, and transport depots. Any operation with a fixed site and regular fuel consumption can benefit from a managed on-site solution.

Yes. IOR can deliver fuel directly to remote sites by rigid tanker, semi, or road train. For very remote or access-restricted sites, IOR works with you to design a delivery schedule and storage solution that matches your access constraints.

On-site refuelling includes IOR-owned infrastructure (tanks, dispensing equipment, HyDip™ FMS) installed at your site, with IOR managing replenishment. Bulk diesel delivery is a scheduled delivery service to your existing storage infrastructure. Many customers use both.

No. Under IOR’s on-site refuelling arrangement, IOR owns and maintains the tank. You pay only for the fuel you consume. This removes the capital cost, compliance burden, and maintenance responsibility from your business.
