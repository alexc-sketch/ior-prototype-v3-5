What is AdBlue®?

Protect your engines. Meet your emissions obligations.

AdBlue® (also known as Diesel Exhaust Fluid or DEF) is a high-purity aqueous urea solution used in Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) systems to reduce harmful NOx emissions from diesel engines. Modern Euro 5 and Euro 6 diesel vehicles and equipment require a continuous, reliable supply of AdBlue® to operate legally and efficiently.

Running out of AdBlue® can trigger engine derate or shutdown — causing costly downtime. HyBlue™ by IOR ensures your supply is always available, in the right format, delivered when and where you need it.

  • Required by all Euro 5 and Euro 6 diesel vehicles and equipment
  • Engine derate or shutdown if supply runs out
  • Must meet ISO 22241 purity standards to protect SCR systems
  • Consumption typically 3–5% of diesel usage

Every batch, every time.

  • 32.5% urea concentration, precisely controlled

HyBlue™ AdBlue® — Common Questions

AdBlue® is the registered trademark for Diesel Exhaust Fluid (DEF) used in SCR systems. HyBlue™ is IOR's branded AdBlue® product, manufactured to ISO 22241 specification. All three terms refer to the same product: a 32.5% aqueous urea solution used to reduce NOx emissions from diesel engines.

AdBlue® consumption is typically 3–5% of diesel consumption, though this varies by engine type, load, and operating conditions. For example, a vehicle consuming 100L of diesel per day will typically consume 3–5L of AdBlue®. Contact our team for a consumption estimate based on your specific fleet.

Using substandard or contaminated AdBlue® can cause crystallisation in your SCR system, damage sensors and injectors, and void your OEM warranty. HyBlue™ is ISO 22241 certified and batch-tested to ensure correct urea concentration and purity levels, protecting your equipment and warranty.

Yes. IOR can coordinate diesel and AdBlue® deliveries on the same schedule. We also supply combination diesel + AdBlue® self-bunded tanks for sites that want to manage both products from a single infrastructure solution.

AdBlue® should be stored in a cool, shaded location away from direct sunlight, ideally between -11°C and +25°C. It must be stored in dedicated AdBlue® containers — never in containers previously used for diesel or other chemicals. IOR can supply dedicated AdBlue® storage tanks and IBCs to ensure correct storage on your site.
