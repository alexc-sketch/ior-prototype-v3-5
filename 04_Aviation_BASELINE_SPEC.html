Compliance & Quality Assurance

IOR's JIG compliance framework, explained.

Every litre of aviation fuel dispensed by IOR is subject to a rigorous quality assurance process from terminal to aircraft. Here's how it works.

The Joint Inspection Group (JIG) is an international organisation that sets the standards for aviation fuel quality and safety. JIG accreditation means that IOR's aviation fuel operations have been independently audited and verified to meet these global standards.

  • JIG standards are recognised by all major airlines and aviation authorities
  • Accreditation requires regular third-party audits and ongoing compliance
  • JIG-compliant fuel is a requirement for many commercial aviation contracts
  • IOR maintains full JIG accreditation across all into-plane refuelling operations

IOR's chain-of-custody process ensures that aviation fuel quality is verified and documented at every stage of the supply chain — from the import terminal to the aircraft wing.

  • Fuel quality tested and certified at the import terminal
  • Dedicated aviation tankers eliminate switch loading and contamination risk
  • Purpose-built storage tanks with regular quality testing
  • Filtration and water separation at every transfer point
  • Full documentation and traceability for every delivery

All IOR aviation operators complete comprehensive training in aviation fuel handling, safety procedures, and aircraft-specific refuelling protocols before working airside.

  • Initial training to JIG and CASA standards
  • Aircraft-type specific refuelling procedures and sign-off
  • Annual recertification and competency assessment
  • Emergency response and spill management training
  • Security clearance and airside access certification

IOR provides comprehensive compliance documentation and reporting to airport operators, including delivery records, quality certificates, and audit reports.

  • Delivery dockets with fuel quality certificates for every uplift
  • Regular compliance reports available via the IOR Customer Portal
  • Third-party audit reports available on request
  • Incident and near-miss reporting with corrective action tracking
  • Annual compliance review meetings with airport management

AeroShell Piston Engine Oils

AeroShell piston engine oils are specifically formulated for aircraft piston engines — providing superior protection against wear, corrosion, and deposit formation.

  • AeroShell W100 — Single-grade mineral oil for air-cooled piston engines. SAE 50 viscosity grade.
  • AeroShell W100 Plus — Mineral oil with anti-wear additive. Recommended for engines with high wear history.
  • AeroShell W15W-50 — Multi-grade mineral oil. Excellent cold-start protection. SAE 15W-50 viscosity grade.
  • AeroShell Oil Sport Plus 4 — Semi-synthetic oil for modern 4-stroke sport aircraft engines.
  • AeroShell Oil Sport Plus 2 — Semi-synthetic oil for 2-stroke sport aircraft engines.
DATA NOTE Product specifications sourced from Shell AeroShell product data sheets. Confirm current product range and availability with IOR technical team before publication.

AEROSHELL TURBINE ENGINE OIL
IMAGE PLACEHOLDER

AeroShell Turbine Engine Oils

AeroShell turbine engine oils are formulated to meet the demanding requirements of gas turbine engines — providing exceptional thermal stability and load-carrying capacity.

  • AeroShell Turbine Oil 500 — Synthetic turbine oil. Meets MIL-PRF-23699F HTS specification. For most turbine-powered aircraft.
  • AeroShell Turbine Oil 560 — Synthetic turbine oil with improved thermal stability. Meets MIL-PRF-23699F STD specification.
  • AeroShell Turbine Oil 390 — Synthetic turbine oil for older turbine engines requiring Type I oil.
  • AeroShell Turbine Oil 750 — High-performance synthetic turbine oil for the most demanding applications.
DATA NOTE Product specifications sourced from Shell AeroShell product data sheets. Confirm current product range and availability with IOR technical team before publication.

AEROSHELL GREASE & FLUID

AeroShell Greases & Fluids

AeroShell greases and hydraulic fluids are formulated for the specific requirements of aircraft airframe and hydraulic systems — providing reliable protection in extreme conditions.

  • AeroShell Grease 33 — Multi-purpose airframe grease. Excellent low-temperature performance. MIL-PRF-23827C.
  • AeroShell Grease 22 — Multi-purpose airframe grease with corrosion inhibitor. MIL-PRF-81322G.
  • AeroShell Fluid 41 — Mineral hydraulic fluid for aircraft hydraulic systems. MIL-PRF-5606H.
  • AeroShell Fluid 31 — Synthetic hydraulic fluid for high-performance aircraft. MIL-PRF-83282D.
